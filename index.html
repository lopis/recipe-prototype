Nitrogen Fixers

Healthy soil needs, among other things, needs nitrogenous compounds. In modern conventional agriculture, these usually come from chemical fertilizers. However, certain food crops form a symbiotic relationship with bacteria which have the ability to form convert the nitrogen from the air into useful organic compounds. Learn more.

Perenial foods

Anual crops give us food once per year and then die off. They then need to grow again from seed. Perenial crops give us food once or more per year, and continue to do so as long as the plant is healthy. More mature plants have more established root systems, are more resistant to pests and droughts, and together they can shape a landscape over the years, help retain water, and improve the quality of the soil by preventing its erosion. Learn More.

Perenial crops include vegetables like Rhubarb, Asparagus and Horseradish, as well as fruit trees and berries.
